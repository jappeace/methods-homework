\subsection{Where can cont not be pushed up?}
If the Q is being split up on both sides of the proof, for example:
\begin{prooftree}

\AxiomC{$
	\Gamma, Q \vdash A \times B
$}
\AxiomC{$
	\Delta, Q, A, B \vdash C
$}
\RightLabel{$E\times$}
\BinaryInfC{$
	\Gamma, \Delta, Q, Q \vdash C
$}
\RightLabel{Cont}
\UnaryInfC{$
	\Gamma, \Delta, Q \vdash C
$}
\end{prooftree}

Now cont can't be pushed up because one side would have nothing to cont.

\section{No noun phrase}
I don't know how to even begin with this using sequant calculi, so
I begin with natural deduction. \\
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
	word & type \\ \toprule
	the & $np/n$ \\
	book & $n$ \\
	that & $(n\setminus n) /(s \setminus np)$ \\
	Allice & $np$ \\
	read & $(np \setminus s) / np$ \\
	yesterday & $s \setminus s$ \\
\end{tabular}

\begin{prooftree}
	\AxiomC{that}
	\UnaryInfC{$
		(n\setminus n) /(s \setminus np) \Rightarrow
		(n\setminus n) /(s \setminus np)
	$}
	\AxiomC{book}
	\UnaryInfC{$
		n \Rightarrow n
	$}
	\AxiomC{the}
	\UnaryInfC{$
		np/n \Rightarrow np/n
	$}
	\RightLabel{$L/$}
	\BinaryInfC{$
		[(np/n/n, n)] \Rightarrow np/n
	$}
	\RightLabel{$L/$}
	\BinaryInfC{$
		[(np/n/n/((n\setminus n) /(s \setminus np)), (n\setminus n) /(s \setminus np), n)] \Rightarrow np/n
	$}
\end{prooftree}
